01 — Lead Sources
Where to find your 50 leads/month
Five sourcing channels across Washington and Oregon. Each gives you publicly available contractor and project data — searchable, exportable, and fresh enough to be actionable.
Primary · Washington
WA L&I Contractor Registry

The Washington State Department of Labor & Industries maintains a public database of every licensed contractor in the state. Searchable by business name, license number, UBI, or specialty. Includes contact info, bond status, and license type.

secure.lni.wa.gov/verify
  • 1.Search by city: "Vancouver" or "Battle Ground" or "Camas"
  • 2.Filter by license type: General Contractor, Specialty (electrical, framing, MEP)
  • 3.Export name, phone, UBI, license status
  • 4.Filter OUT sole proprietors under $1M — focus on companies with 5+ employees
  • 5.~800 active GC licenses in Clark County alone
Primary · Clark County
Clark County Permit Database (LMS)

Clark County's public LMS permit system lets you search permits by contractor name, date range, or project type. Commercial permits over $500K filed in the last 90 days = active GCs with active projects = perfect timing for a Confluence AI pitch.

permits.clark.wa.gov/pub/lms
  • 1.Search by date range: last 60–90 days
  • 2.Filter permit type: Commercial New Construction, Commercial TI
  • 3.Filter by valuation: $250K+ projects
  • 4.Cross-reference contractor name → L&I lookup → get phone
  • 5.~15–20 qualified prospects/month from this source alone
Primary · Oregon
Oregon CCB Registry

Oregon's Construction Contractors Board maintains 45,000+ active licenses. The Open Data Portal syncs daily. ccblookup.com adds a trust score, bond/insurance expiry, and Google Business match on top of the raw state data.

search.ccb.oregon.gov ccblookup.com (Multnomah, Washington, Clackamas counties)
  • 1.Filter by county: Multnomah, Washington, Clackamas
  • 2.License type: Residential GC, Commercial GC, Developer
  • 3.Use ccblookup.com Trust Score >70 as baseline quality filter
  • 4.Cross-reference with Oregon Secretary of State for company size signal
  • 5.~20–25 qualifying prospects/month from Portland metro
Secondary · Intent Signal
City of Vancouver Permit Portal

Vancouver WA city permits (separate from Clark County unincorporated) tracked via Accela Citizen Access. Best for catching mid-size commercial projects inside city limits — these GCs are often overlooked by the larger data aggregators.

cityofvancouver.us/permits
  • 1.Search commercial permits $200K+ last 90 days
  • 2.Extract contractor of record from each permit
  • 3.This is the highest-intent signal: active project = active pain
  • 4.~5–8 high-intent leads/month
Secondary · Enrichment
LinkedIn + Google Maps Enrichment

After pulling raw lists from L&I and CCB, enrich with LinkedIn (find the owner or operations director) and Google Maps (verify company size, read reviews for pain-point signals like "disorganized," "slow invoicing"). Free, 10 min/prospect.

LinkedIn: GC search · Vancouver, WA
  • 1.Search "general contractor Vancouver WA" — filter 10–50 employees
  • 2.Find: Owner, Operations Director, VP Construction, Project Controls
  • 3.Google Maps reviews: look for "hard to get updates," "scheduling issues" = pain signal
  • 4.Add direct name + title to CRM before AI call is made
Paid Option · Scale
Dodge Data / ConstructConnect

For when you want to scale beyond 50 leads/month. Dodge tracks every commercial project in design, permitting, and bidding phase across the Pacific Northwest — with contractor contact data pre-attached. ~$200–$400/month.

dodgedata.com
  • 1.Filter: WA + OR, project value $500K–$20M, stage: Bidding or Awarded
  • 2.GC and owner contact data pre-attached to each project
  • 3.Best for developer/owner segment — harder to reach via permit data
  • 4.Start free sources first; activate Dodge at 30+ leads/month

03 — NLPearl AI Call Scripts
Word-for-word scripts with branching
Three scripts: cold prospecting (GC version), voicemail drop, and re-engagement. Each is written for NLPearl's natural language format — no stiff IVR language. The AI sounds like a knowledgeable project coordinator, not a sales bot.
NLPearl Configuration Notes Voice persona: Professional, calm, direct — not salesy. Name: "Alex from Confluence AI." Speed: slightly slower than default (construction decision-makers often are on a jobsite or in a truck). Interruption handling: ON. Call recording: ON. Webhook to Google Sheets + CRM: required. Max call length: 4 minutes for cold, 2 minutes for voicemail.

Script 1 — Cold Prospecting · General Contractor

Target: Owner, Operations Director, or VP of Construction at a GC running 3+ concurrent projects

// COLD CALL · GC VERSION · MAX 3.5 MIN NLPearl AI — "Alex"
// OPENING — confident, not apologetic
AI: "Hi, this is Alex calling from Confluence AI — is this [FIRST_NAME]?"
// BRANCH: Yes, this is them →
AI: "Perfect. I'll keep this short. We work with general contractors in the Vancouver and Portland metro area — specifically helping them get real-time visibility into job costs and schedules across multiple projects. Most of the GCs we talk to are managing five, six, eight jobs and finding out about cost problems after the project closes. Does that sound familiar at all?"
// BRANCH: "Yes" / "Absolutely" / "Unfortunately" →
AI: "That's exactly the problem we solve. We build what we call a Command Center — a live dashboard that connects your Procore, Sage, or whatever system you're using, and shows you margin, schedule, and subcontractor performance in real time. One of our clients caught a $180,000 overrun in week two of having it live — before it became a loss. I'm not trying to sell you anything on this call. I just want to find out if the timing makes sense for a 30-minute conversation with our founder. He's local — born and raised in Vancouver. Would sometime next week work?"
// BRANCH: "What software do you use / what exactly do you build?" →
AI: "Great question. We build on top of whatever you already have — Procore, Sage 300, QuickBooks, Buildertrend. We don't replace anything, we connect it all so you can see the full picture. No new software to learn, no migration. The whole thing is typically live in 6–8 weeks on a fixed fee. Does it make sense to get 30 minutes on the calendar so our founder can show you what it looks like for a company your size?"
// BRANCH: "We already have Procore" →
AI: "Yeah, Procore is great for project management — most of our clients use it. The problem is Procore doesn't give you a live margin picture across your whole portfolio, and it doesn't connect to your accounting system automatically. That's the gap we fill. Would it be worth 30 minutes to see what that looks like?"
// BRANCH: "Not interested" / "Too busy" →
AI: "Completely understand — I'll let you go. Would it be alright if I send a quick email with what we do? No pitch, just two paragraphs and a case study so you have it if timing changes."
// BRANCH: "Sure, send an email" →
AI: "Perfect. What's the best email? I'll have it to you within the hour. And just to make sure I send the most relevant info — roughly how many projects are you running right now?"
// BRANCH: Appointment booked →
AI: "Excellent. I'm going to send you a calendar link and a short 5-question form right now — takes about 3 minutes. It just helps our founder prepare so the call is worth your time. You'll also get a reminder the day before. Does [NAME] at this number work for the confirmation text?"
// WRONG PERSON / GATEKEEPER →
AI: "No problem at all. Who would be the right person to talk to about operations or project management systems? Is that the owner, or someone else?"
// If name given → "Great, I'll try [NAME] directly. Thank you." → log new contact in CRM
// If no name given → "Got it. I'll reach back out another time. Thanks for your help."

Script 2 — Voicemail Drop

Fires automatically when NLPearl detects voicemail. Max 28 seconds.

// VOICEMAIL · 28 SECONDS MAX · NO CALLBACK NUMBER — use email only
AI: "Hi [FIRST_NAME], this is Alex from Confluence AI in Vancouver. We help general contractors in the Portland-Vancouver area get real-time visibility into job costs and schedules across multiple projects — most of our clients catch expensive overruns before they close. I'll send you a quick email in the next hour. No pitch, just a two-minute case study. Talk soon."
// DO NOT leave a callback number — forces them to read the email, not call a number they'll ignore
// Trigger: send email within 5 minutes of voicemail drop (automated via NLPearl webhook → Gmail)

Script 3 — Cold Prospecting · Subcontractor Version

Different pain point emphasis — subs care about getting paid on time and winning more bids

// COLD CALL · SUBCONTRACTOR VERSION · MAX 3 MIN
AI: "Hi [FIRST_NAME], Alex from Confluence AI in Vancouver. I'll keep this quick — we work with specialty contractors in the Portland metro helping them track which GCs pay on time, which jobs are actually profitable, and how to use their past project data to bid smarter. Is that something that's on your radar right now?"
// BRANCH: "Yes, payment timing is always an issue" →
AI: "Exactly. We build a simple dashboard that tracks every GC you've worked with — payment timing, project profitability, change order history. So the next time you're deciding whether to bid a job, you have data instead of gut feel. The whole thing connects to whatever accounting system you use — QuickBooks, Sage, whatever. Would it be worth 30 minutes to see what that looks like? Our founder is local — he's been on the other side of that phone call chasing payments."
// BRANCH: "How much does it cost?" →
AI: "It's a fixed-fee engagement — we scope it out based on your setup in a free 30-minute call. Most sub-focused builds come in well under what a single slow-paying GC costs you in a year. Worth a conversation?"

Script 4 — Re-engagement (Day 8 Follow-up)

For contacts who were connected but didn't book. Reference the previous call.

// RE-ENGAGEMENT · DAY 8 · SHORT · REFERENCE PRIOR CONTACT
AI: "Hi [FIRST_NAME], Alex again from Confluence AI — we spoke briefly about a week ago about real-time cost visibility for your projects. I wanted to follow up quickly. We actually just wrapped a build for a Clark County GC — they caught a $180K overrun in the first two weeks. Thought that might be timely for you. Is 30 minutes still worth a look, or should I check back in a month or two?"
// BRANCH: "Check back in a few months" →
AI: "Absolutely. I'll put a note to reach out in [MONTH+2]. Is there a specific project or timing that would make it more relevant — like the start of a new fiscal year or a big job kicking off?"
// Log future follow-up date in CRM. Move to "Nurture" pipeline stage.

05 — Pre-Call Questionnaire
Sent automatically after appointment booking
NLPearl fires a webhook when an appointment is booked → Google Forms link is sent via automated Gmail. The form takes 3 minutes to complete. Responses land in a Google Sheet that syncs to the CRM. Founder reviews answers before the call — no wasted 30 minutes asking basics.
Automation setup (Google Workspace) NLPearl webhook → Google Apps Script → (1) send form link via Gmail, (2) create CRM contact, (3) add Google Calendar event with form response link in description. Use Cal.com or Google Calendar for scheduling — embed the link in NLPearl's booking confirmation message.
